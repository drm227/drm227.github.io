Leading Public Library

Two hundred years ago there was no such thing as a public library, in Boston or any other American city or town. Public libraries in America were a 19th century development. Boston was at the forefront of the movement. The Boston Public Library was founded in 1848. The BPF was the first municipally funded free public library. In 1855 it moved into a new buidling of its own. That was on Boylston Street, across from the Common, where the Colonial Theater is today. When the American Library Association was founded in 18??, the librarian of the Boston Public Library was chosen to be its first president. The BPL was the first public library to bring books to its patrons in residential neighborhoods,

Sargent

McKim selected John Singer Sargent to do the murals for this space. Sargent was born in Europe in 1856 to American parents. Although an American citizen, he never lived in the United States ??? Sargent started his career as a painter in Paris in 1874. He moved to London in 1884. His first solo exhibition was actually here in Boston in 1888.

By the time McKim approached him in 1890, he was an established portrait painter. Painting portraits was lower on the the artistic pecking order than painting murals. Sargent welcomed the opportunity to create a larger work that would make his mark as an artist.

Commission

Sargent worked on these murals from 1890 to 1919. They were completed in four phases. The north alcove and ceiling vault were installed in 1895. The south alcove was installed in 1903. The south ceiling vault and lunettes were installed in 1916. The two panels on either end of the stairway were installed in 1919.

As with the other murals, the choice of subject was left to the artist. Sargent chose the history of religion. The murals were intended to be eductional, rather in contrast to the devotional murals by LaFarge in Trinity Church.

Sargent abandoned the project before it was completed. He had planned to paint the Sermon on the Mount on that large panel above the stairway. why??? Sargent died in 1925 in London.
