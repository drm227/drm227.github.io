Boston By Foot

My name is Dennis. I am a volunteer tour guide for Boston By Foot. We give walking tours of Boston, talking about its history and architecture.

What is architecture?

Boston was founded in 1630, almost 400 years ago! That's very old for an American city. So Boston has lots of history, and many old buildings. We are going to look at some of them, and talk about their role in Boston history.

Faneuil Hall

What is the name of the building behind Samuel Adams? Where does the name come from?

stretchers ... headers ... Flemish bond ... alternating stretchers and headers ... headers centered on stretchers above and below

most popular place in Boston ... almost as many people come here as go to Disney

Boston Massacre

What was the Boston massacre?

on March 5, 1770, British soldiers fired into a crowd of protestors, killing four and wounding seven ... first one was Crispuse Attucks, a former enslaved African

Why were there British soldiers in Boston?

Stamp Act Riots ... Thomas Hutchinson's house ransacked ... more taxes in 1868 ... royal governor requested troops to help enforce them ... troops arrived in 1868

people resented soldiers presence

Why did they shoot?

argument between an apprentice and a British officer about unpaid bill ... crowd gathers and harrasses lone sentry in front of custom house

fight at ropewalk two days before ... gang armed with clubs roaming Boston looking for a fight with soldiers ... stumbles into crowd around sentry

bells rung ... British officer in charge arrives with soldiers ... situation escalates ... crowd starts throwing rocks

soldier knocked down ... fires gun while getting up ... other soldiers fire ...

What happened to the British soldiers who shot people?

tried for murder ... trial held here in Old State House ... John Adams was their lawyer ... two found guilty of manslaughter and branded

Tea Party

What was the most famous meeting held here? (hint: Samuel Adams was a member)

protest meeting against new tax on tea ... December 16, 1773

What happened that day? Before they threw the tea in the harbor?

Tea Act 1773 ... Dartmouth arrives in November with tea ... opponents wanted the ship to be sent back with the tea ... Governor Hutchinson did not agree

Samuel Adams organized a protest meeting at Old South ... more people came than would fit ... 25 men were assigned to watch the ship and prevent the tea from being unloaded

delegation sent to Hutchinson ... came back reporting Hutchinson again refused ... Adams says "This meeting can do nothing further to save the country."

later story says it was a signal ... about 100 men diguised as Indians went to the ships ... smashed open 342 chests of tea and dumped them into harbor

How did the King and Parliament react?

British make an example of ... harbor closed ... Hutchinson replaced by Gen. Gage
