MIT History

chartered in 1861; started classes in 1862 in rented space in Boston; moved into new building of their own in Back Bay in 1865; architecture program started same year, oldest in U.S.

known as Boston Tech; commuter school with 1500 students

moved to new campus in 1916 with elaborate ceremonies; New Tech; celebrated 100th anniversary in June

now 4500 undergraduate, 6800 graduate, 3700 researchers; UROP

The Alchemist

Juame Plensa, installed 2010

"Alchemist is related to Plensa’s other works, Nomade (2010) and El Alma Del Ebro (2010), which are made of randomly arranged stainless steel letters of the alphabet, painted white and arranged in the shape of a person sitting with knees drawn up to the chest."

"However, in the place of letters of the alphabet, Plensa’s work for MIT is created from numeric symbols, as an “homage to all the researchers and scientists“ that have contributed to scientific and mathematical knowledge."

Kresge Auditorium

Eero Saarinen, 1953-1955, (St. Louis Gateway Arch)

thin shell reinforced concrete dome is one-eighth of a sphere resting on three support points

50 feet tall at center' structural layer varies from 3 to 7 inches thick

originally white (fine limestone chips in a liquid acrylic polymer binder); series of problems and repairs; copper roofing from 1979

interior structurally independent of dome; 1200 seat theater, no support beams obstructing views

lower level with smaller theater, rehearsal rooms, offices

Simmons Hall

Steven Holl, 2000-2002

10 story building wrapped with 250 windows; Each residential room has a tic-tac-toe pattern of nine operable windows

Each one story high pre-cast section is five columns wide

The structural engineers color coded their drawings to indicate the differences in structural reinforcement in each section and the architect was so impressed with the look of the drawings that he decided to replicate this color scheme on the aluminum cladding.

the architect designed all of the furniture within the rooms.

close to 40% of the space in this building is devoted to community use. Holl has created a series of community spaces throughout the building which penetrate several floors and provide dramatic cave like quarters for students to gather for study and socialization.

Bosworth

graduate MIT 1889; worked as draftsman for H.H. Richardson while student; first worked for Olmstead on Stanford campus plan; studied at the famous École des Beaux-Arts 1896-1900; appointed architect 1913

Layout

dome inspire by Pantheon in Rome

campus oriented towards river; courtyard intended to terrace down to river; buildings step up in height towards center

statue of Minerva

intended to be the center of activity, but is not

courtyard vs infinite corridor orientation

Construction

steel and concrete with limestone facing

compare exterior with BPL; names engraved

functional interior, more like industrial design

Beaux Arts vs Skyscrapers

Beaux Arts aesthetic and Boston

skyscraper style and aesthetic in Chicago; Louis Sullivan

Boston vs Skyscraper; 1891 height restriction; architects role in law and 1928 change; Paul Dirac quote

exterior Beaux Arts vs functional exterior

Collier Memorial

J. Meejin Yoon, 2015

in honor of Officer Sean Collier who was shot and killed on April 18th 2013 by Boston Marathon bombers

composed of thirty-two solid blocks of granite that form a five-way stone vault. Each block supports the other to create a central, covered space for reflection

The design relies on the exact fit of the 32 stone blocks to transfer loads in pure compression from stone to stone.

http://www.architectmagazine.com/design/buildings/the-making-of-mits-collier-memorial_o

Brain & Cognitive Sciences

Goody, Clancy & Associates and Charles Correa Associates, 2005

411,000 square feet of space, contains the largest neurosciences center in the world

at-grade tunnel 50 feet wide and 22 feet high has been incorporated into the design of the building, which had to be completely isolated from the vibrations of the train tracks

The main floor of the building is at the level above the train tunnel and the atrium rises from that floor to the roof.

Tiles

Bars of Colors within Squares, Sol LeWitt, 2007

LeWitt was a founding father of the Conceptual art movement in the early and middle 1960s.

commissioned in 2005, completed in 2007 after his death

5500 square feet

15 18-foot squares of brightly colored geometric patterns set into a white frame and then suspended in a gray field

3 primary colors: red, yellow blue; 3 secondary colors: orange, purple, grean

aggregate is composed of chips of recycled glass suspended in specifically colored epoxy-resins

Great Dome

Bosworth design had large assembly hall under dome; due to lack of funds, space was partitioned into lobby, lecture hall (10-250) and library

main reading room, 5th floor of building 10, inside Great Dome

Pantheon in Rome; 100 foot diameter dome; rotunda is 75 feet wide and high; the two meet in a 27 foot diameter oculus (same as Pantheon); enclosed by two layers of glass

two layers of glass, couple feet apart; consisting of many small glass blocks, acting like a Fresnel lens

oculus covered during WWII; restoration in 2013

https://libraries.mit.edu/barker/about/, http://news.mit.edu/2013/great-dome-reborn-oculus-0215
