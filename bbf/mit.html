Tiles

Bars of Colors within Squares, Sol LeWitt, 2007

LeWitt was a founding father of Conceptual art, an artistic movement in the early and middle 1960s.

commissioned in 2005, completed after his death in 2007

"The tiles use only six colors: three primary colors and an additional six secondary colors created from the primaries. All the squares include all the colors, set into a white frame and then suspended in a gray field."

" the aggregate is composed of chips of recycled glass suspended in specifically colored epoxy-resins"

https://listart.mit.edu/sites/default/files/MIT_PW_LeWitt.pdf

Great Dome

Bosworth design had large assembly hall under dome; due to lack of funds, space was partitioned into lobby, lecture hall (10-250) and library

main reading room, 5th floor of building 10, inside Great Dome

Pantheon in Rome; 100 foot diameter dome; rotunda is 75 feet wide and high; the two meet in a 27 foot diameter oculus (same as Pantheon); enclosed by two layers of glass

two layers of glass, couple feet apart; consisting of many small glass blocks, acting like a Fresnel lens

oculus covered during WWII; restoration in 2013

https://libraries.mit.edu/barker/about/, http://news.mit.edu/2013/great-dome-reborn-oculus-0215
