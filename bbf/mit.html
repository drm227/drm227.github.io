Kresge Auditorium

Eero Saarinen, 1953-1955

shape of the building recalls the neoclassical domes of MacClaurin and Rogers buildings

shell of the building, one-eighth of a sphere, floats free of the interior resting on three main support points

The building roof is supported in only three places and is 3.5 inches thick in the middle and rises to a height of 50 feet.

"The interior of the building, structurally independent of the roof, contains a 1200 seat auditorium that has no obstructed views as there are no interior supports. There is a smaller theater that seats approximately 200 as well as rehearsal rooms, dressing rooms, offices and lounges downstairs."

"Keeping the dome watertight proved to be a difficult problem. Initially coated with a liquid applied waterproofing material, the building experienced continual leak problems due to expansion and contraction of the dome surface and numerous re-roofing schemes were tried until the present installation of copper shingles was installed in the 1960s."

http://www.archdaily.com/492176/ad-classics-kresge-auditorium-eero-saarinen-and-associates

MIT Chapel

Eero Saarinen (St. Louis Gateway Arch), 1954

"the windowless chapel has a rectangular narthex connected to a cylindrical chapel this is surrounded by a moat. The exterior brick contrasts with the concrete, steel, and glass of the other buildings in the oval."

"The primary light sources are external. During the summer, when water is in the moat, the sunlight is reflected upward off the water through openings under the top of the brick arches at the base of the building, casting sparkling dots of light on the interior walls. In addition, light pours down from above thus illuminating from above and below the windowless chapel."

Simmons Hall

Steven Holl, 2000-2002

10 story building wrapped with 250 windows; Each residential room has a tic-tac-toe pattern of nine operable windows

Each one story high pre-cast section is five columns wide

The structural engineers color coded their drawings to indicate the differences in structural reinforcement in each section and the architect was so impressed with the look of the drawings that he decided to replicate this color scheme on the aluminum cladding.

the architect designed all of the furniture within the rooms.

close to 40% of the space in this building is devoted to community use. Holl has created a series of community spaces throughout the building which penetrate several floors and provide dramatic cave like quarters for students to gather for study and socialization.

Great Dome

Bosworth design had large assembly hall under dome; due to lack of funds, space was partitioned into lobby, lecture hall (10-250) and library

main reading room, 5th floor of building 10, inside Great Dome

Pantheon in Rome; 100 foot diameter dome; rotunda is 75 feet wide and high; the two meet in a 27 foot diameter oculus (same as Pantheon); enclosed by two layers of glass

two layers of glass, couple feet apart; consisting of many small glass blocks, acting like a Fresnel lens

oculus covered during WWII; restoration in 2013

https://libraries.mit.edu/barker/about/, http://news.mit.edu/2013/great-dome-reborn-oculus-0215
