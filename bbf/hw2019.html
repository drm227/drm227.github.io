Colonial Waterfront

C shape between Rowes Wharf and Lewis Wharf ... piecemeal landmaking by individual landowners ... wharfing out

two larger projects changed appearance of cove ... Barricado 1670's ... defensive barrier ... fell into disrepair in 1700's

Long Wharf 1711-1715 ... 1600 ft long ... started at Chatam Row ... end in deep water, beyond Barricado ... shorter now due to landmaking

19th Century Landmaking

larger scale landmaking projects ... new streets ... south, west and north sections of cove

Atlantic Ave {roject the last and largest ... creates street along path of Barricado between Rowes and Lewis ... we will follow that today

Atlantic Ave Project ... problem ... solution ... Union Freight Railroad 1873-1970

Fort Hill Project ... problem ... solution

20th Century Decline and Redevelopment

Great Depression does not end after WWII in Boston ... extends into early 1960's ... collapse of industry ... population decline during 1950's ... 1959 bond rating ... America's first rust belt city ... downtown waterfront wharves fall into disuse ... some converted to parking lots

recovery period starts in 1960's and extends into 1980's ... today's waterfront created in that period ... mix of new construction and conversion of old buildings - adaptive reuse ...

era of large urban renewal projects ... comparison to Government Center and Quincy Market Downtown Waterfront Redevelopment Project ... scope in space and time ... moved Atlantic Avenue inland ... we'll follow old Atlantic Avenue

East Boston

no such place in Colonial times ... five islands used by farmers to raise hay and graze livestock

1833 William Sumner forms East Boston Company ... lays out street grid ... starts landmaking

develops quickly with ship building ... population grows from 8 in 1833 to over 15,000 in 1855

Donald McKay and clipper ships ... translantic service to Cunard Line's pier in 1840

Logan Airport a 20th century addition ... opened in 1923 on 134 acres of made land ... now has 1413 acres, mostly from 1940's

FAA height restriction
